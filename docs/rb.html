Report Builder

Introduction

The Chartbeat Report Builder is designed for analysts and researchers to create and subscribe to custom historical reports about their audience and content. While the Dashboard excels at presenting real-time actionable data, the Report Builder offers the chance to take your data one step further.

The tool offers a wide array of metrics, filters, and groups that can be used by both analytics and editorial teams to track audience and content data over time. With the Report Builder interface, not only can you elect to receive these reports in your inbox, but you’ll have access to an archive of all the reports you’ve created. It’s easy to copy this data into your own pivot tables to organize your data however you need.

Get started at chartbeat.com/report/builder.

Required Parameters

Report Type

There are two report types to pick from: a one time report, which creates a single CSV file to be downloaded or sent via email, and a recurring report – which you can subscribe to and receive it in your inbox on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis. Note that daily reports look at the past day of data, weekly reports look at the past week, and monthly look at the past month.

Date Range

The date range specifies time frame you want to pull from for your report. You can select a preset range of the past 7 days, 31 days, the previous 24 hours, or a you can create a custom date range with the calendar box.

Site

The site field is the domain that you want to create a report for. Note that you may only select domains that your Chartbeat login has access to.

Metrics

The metrics parameter identifies which audience and site data points you want returned in your report. Any given query may include multiple metrics to create comprehensive reports.

Average Scroll The average maximum depth that visitors have scrolled to, in pixels, from the top of the page.
Average Engaged Time The average amount of time in seconds visitors actively spent on a page.
Scroll Starts The percentage of page views where a scroll event occurred, expressed as a decimal.
Total Engaged Time The total amount of time in seconds spent actively on a page across sites.
Unique Cookies A count of the number of unique cookies who visited the page.
Page Views A count of the number of page views.

Filter

Adding a filter to your report allows you to specify a variable you’d like to focus on. Use the filter parameter to dive deeper into your historical metrics and create reports that reflect specific audience breakdowns.
